Evil Text Editor Kata

You've been asked to build a single line text editor, but the client can only afford to pay for six features. They can't make up their mind though, so they won't tell you what each feature is until you've completed the previous one. The purpose of this kata is to embrace change, so keep your design simple enough that it can respond to this change easily.

All you know at the beginning of the project is that the editor will have a buffer and a cursor, don't worry about a graphical component. You can assume that the buffer has some starting text. Click the button below once you're ready for your first feature.
